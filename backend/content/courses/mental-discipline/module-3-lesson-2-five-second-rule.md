# Overcoming Resistance — Lesson 2: The 5-Second Rule

**Module 3 of 5 | Day 8 | Time: 8 min"

---

## What You'll Learn
A 5-second tool that bypasses your brain's resistance and gets you moving.

---

## The Science

When you have an impulse to act on a goal, your brain takes about 5 seconds to process it. If you don't physically move within those 5 seconds, your brain's "safety mechanism" kicks in and talks you out of it.

**The impulse → hesitation → rationalization cycle:**

1. You think: "I should get up and exercise" (impulse)
2. You hesitate: "But I'm comfortable" (0.5 seconds)
3. You rationalize: "I'll do it later / I'm too tired / it's cold" (5 seconds)
4. You don't do it

---

## The Rule

**Count backward: 5-4-3-2-1-GO — and physically move.**

- 5: See the impulse
- 4: Acknowledge it
- 3: Prepare to act
- 2: Commit
- 1: Go (physically move)

The counting interrupts your brain's hesitation pattern. The physical movement bypasses the rationalization loop.

---

## When to Use It

- Waking up: "5-4-3-2-1-GO" — swing your legs out of bed
- Starting a task: "5-4-3-2-1-GO" — open the document
- Saying no: "5-4-3-2-1-NO" — decline the thing you don't want
- Difficult conversation: "5-4-3-2-1-GO" — make the call
- Stopping a bad habit: "5-4-3-2-1-STOP" — put down the phone
