## What Deep Work Is

**Deep work:** Professional activities performed in a state of distraction-free concentration that push your cognitive capabilities to their limit.

**Shallow work:** Non-cognitively demanding, logistical-style tasks, often performed while distracted.

Examples:

| Deep Work | Shallow Work |
|-----------|-------------|
| Writing a proposal | Answering emails |
| Learning a new skill | Scheduling meetings |
| Creating a product | Data entry |
| Strategic planning | Status updates |
| Creative problem-solving | Social media browsing |

---

## The 90-Minute Rule

Your brain can do deep work for about 90 minutes before needing a break.

**Deep work session:**
- Choose ONE task
- Remove all distractions (phone off, door closed, notifications off)
- Set timer for 90 minutes
- Work without interruption
- Take a 15-20 minute break

**One 90-minute deep work session = 4 hours of distracted work.**

---

## Scheduling Deep Work

Deep work needs to be scheduled. If it's not on your calendar, it won't happen.

| Strategy | How It Works |
|----------|-------------|
| Time blocking | Schedule 90-min blocks in your calendar |
| Daily rhythm | Same time every day (e.g., 7-8:30am) |
| Phone-free zone | No phone in the room during deep work |
| The shutdown ritual | Clear your workspace, review tomorrow, stop |
