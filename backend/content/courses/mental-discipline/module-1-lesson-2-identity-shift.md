## The Problem with "Should"

"I should exercise."
"I should save money."
"I should start that project."

**Should** is a weak motivator because it's external. It's what you think you *ought* to do.

**Identity** is internal. It's who you *are*.

| Goal | Should Statement | Identity Statement |
|------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Exercise | "I should work out." | "I'm someone who moves daily." |
| Save money | "I should save more." | "I'm someone who builds security." |
| Write | "I should write that book." | "I'm a writer." |

---

## The 2-Word Identity Exercise

Complete this sentence with 2 words:

> "I'm someone who __________ and __________."

**Examples:**
- "I'm someone who **shows up**."
- "I'm someone who **keeps promises** (to myself and others)."
- "I'm someone who **follows through**."

---

## Acting As If

You don't need to fully believe the identity yet. You just need to act as if.

- Act as if you're someone who exercises → eventually you become someone who exercises
- Act as if you're someone who saves → eventually you become a saver
- The belief follows the action, not the other way around
