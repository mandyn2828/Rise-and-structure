## The Myth

Most people believe: *Discipline is something you have or don't have.*

**Reality:** Discipline is not a character trait. It's a system.

Think of it like a muscle:
- It gets tired when you use it too much
- It can be trained and strengthened
- It works best when you don't rely on it all the time

---

## The Discipline Equation

```
Discipline = Environment + Systems + Identity
```

### Environment (50%)
Your surroundings matter more than your willpower. A cookie on the counter will beat your willpower 9 times out of 10. Move the cookie.

### Systems (30%)
Routines, schedules, and habits that run on autopilot. When you have a system, you don't need to decide — you just execute.

### Identity (20%)
"I'm someone who shows up" > "I should show up." Identity-based habits stick because they're not a choice — they're who you are.

---

## The Willpower Battery

Your willpower is like a phone battery:
- Morning: 100%
- Each decision drains it
- By evening: 20% or less

**Strategic implication:** Do your most important thing FIRST. Before you check email, before social media, before the day drains your battery.
