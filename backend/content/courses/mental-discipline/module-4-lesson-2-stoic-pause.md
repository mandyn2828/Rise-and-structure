**Module 4 of 5 | Day 11 | Time: 10 min**

## What You'll Learn
A 10-second tool that prevents you from reacting impulsively — and gives you the power to choose your response.

---

## The Gap

> "Between stimulus and response, there is a space. In that space is our power to choose our response. In our response lies our growth and freedom." — Viktor Frankl

When someone says something that makes you angry, or a frustrating email arrives, or you feel the urge to quit, there's a split second where you can choose your response.

Most people skip this gap. They react instantly:
- Insult → Retaliation
- Craving → Indulgence
- Frustration → Quit
- Distraction → Scroll

---

## The Practice: The Stoic Pause

When something happens that triggers a reaction:

1. **Stop.** Don't respond. Don't reach for the phone. Don't speak.
2. **Breathe.** One deep breath in, one long exhale out.
3. **Count to 10.** Not 3. Not 5. Ten. Let the initial emotional spike pass.
4. **Choose.** Now, with a clearer head, decide your response.

---

## When to Use It

- An email or message that makes you angry
- A craving for something you're trying to avoid
- The urge to quit something difficult
- A difficult conversation
- Before making any decision while emotional

---

## The 24-Hour Rule

For big decisions (quitting a job, ending a relationship, making a major purchase): wait 24 hours. If you still want to do it tomorrow, do it then.

Most urgent feelings aren't urgent. They're just intense.
