## The Cost of Switching

Your brain cannot multitask. It can task-switch very quickly, but every switch costs you:

| Switch | Time Lost |
|--------|-----------|
| Check phone while working | 23 minutes to refocus |
| Switch between 2 projects | 15 minutes to re-engage |
| Respond to a notification | 5 minutes of lost context |
| Check email mid-task | 10 minutes to return to flow |

**Average knowledge worker switches tasks every 3 minutes.** That's 20+ context switches per hour. No wonder you feel scattered.

---

## The Attention Audit

Today, track:

1. How many times did I check my phone while working?
2. How many tabs do I have open right now?
3. How many times did I switch tasks without finishing the first one?
4. How many notifications did I receive?

Just track. Don't judge. Awareness is the first step.

---

## The Single-Tasking Rule

**One task. One screen. One window. Until it's done or 45 minutes pass.**

When you work:
- Close all tabs except the one you need
- Put phone face down, out of reach
- Close email and messaging apps
- Set a timer for 45 minutes
- Don't stop until the timer goes off
