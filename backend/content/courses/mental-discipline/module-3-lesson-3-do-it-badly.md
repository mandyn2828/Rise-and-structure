# Overcoming Resistance — Lesson 3: The "Do It Badly" Permission

**Module 3 of 5 | Day 9 | Time: 10 min"

---

## What You'll Learn
Why perfectionism is the enemy of progress — and how to give yourself permission to do things badly.

---

## The Problem

Perfectionism isn't about having high standards. It's about fear. Fear of judgment. Fear of failure. Fear of not being good enough.

And it manifests as **not starting.**

---

## The Mantra

> **"Done is better than perfect."**

Not "done is perfect." Done is just done. You can improve done. You can't improve not started.

---

## Examples of "Doing It Badly"

| Your Inner Critic Says | "Do It Badly" Version |
|----------------------|---------------------|
| "I need to write a perfect first draft." | "Write a messy first draft. Editing comes later." |
| "I need the perfect gym routine." | "Do 10 jumping jacks. That counts." |
| "I need a full business plan." | "Send one email to a potential client." |
| "I need to meditate properly." | "Sit for 2 minutes and breathe. That's meditation." |
| "I need to eat perfectly." | "Eat one vegetable today. That's progress." |

---

## The 80% Rule

80% of the value comes from 80% of the effort. The last 20% takes 80% of the time.

Most of the time, 80% is good enough.

- 80% of a budget → usable
- 80% of a workout → beneficial
- 80% of a product → sellable
- 80% of a book → publishable

**Ship at 80%. Iterate from there.**
