## What You'll Learn
How doing one uncomfortable thing every day builds mental toughness.

---

## Why Discomfort Matters

Your comfort zone is a trap. The more comfortable you make your life, the less resilient you become.

- When you never push past discomfort, small challenges feel like big ones.
- When you regularly do hard things, hard things become normal.
- Mental toughness is a muscle. You build it by using it.

---

## The Daily Discomfort Practice

**Every day, do one thing that makes you slightly uncomfortable.**

| Category | Examples |
|----------|---------|
| Physical | Cold shower, extra 10 pushups, 2-min plank |
| Mental | Read something challenging, do a puzzle, learn a new word |
| Emotional | Have the hard conversation, say no, ask for help |
| Social | Talk to a stranger, compliment someone, speak up in a meeting |
| Practical | Take the stairs, don't use the remote, walk instead of drive |

---

## The Cold Shower Protocol

One of the most effective discomfort practices:

1. Start your shower normally (warm)
2. At the end, switch to cold
3. Start with 15 seconds
4. Build to 30 seconds, then 60 seconds
5. Breathe deeply. Don't tense up.
6. When the urge to turn it off hits, stay for 5 more seconds.

**Why it works:** It's safe discomfort. You learn that discomfort passes. You learn that you can survive hard things.
