## The Dip

Every worthwhile project follows the same curve:

```
  ▲
  │    ╱
  │   ╱  ╲    ← The Dip (most people quit here)
  │  ╱    ╲__╱
  │ ╱            ╲
  │╱              ╲___
  └─────────────────────────►
  Start                  Mastery
```

1. **The Start:** Excitement. New project. New energy.
2. **The Dip:** The hard middle. The novelty wears off. Progress is slow. You want to quit.
3. **The Plateau:** You've survived the dip. Things get easier. You build momentum.
4. **Mastery:** You're competent, consistent, and the habit is established.

**The dip is where the magic happens.** It's also where 90% of people quit.

---

## How to Know If You're in the Dip

Signs:
- The excitement is gone
- Progress feels slow or invisible
- You're making excuses to skip
- You're comparing yourself to others who are "ahead"
- You're questioning whether this is worth it

---

## How to Survive the Dip

1. **Lower the bar.** The dip isn't the time for peak performance. It's the time for showing up. Minimum viable effort counts.

2. **Focus on input, not output.** Don't worry about results. Worry about showing up. "I did my 5-minute routine" is a win.

3. **Change the metaphor.** You're not climbing a mountain. You're digging a tunnel. Every day, you're one shovelful deeper. You can't see progress day to day, but one day you'll break through.

4. **Tell someone.** Share that you're in the dip. Accountability helps.
