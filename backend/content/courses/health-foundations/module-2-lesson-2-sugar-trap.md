## What You'll Learn
Why your brain is wired to crave sugar at 3pm — and how to break the cycle without feeling deprived.

---

## Why You Crave Sugar

Here's what happens in a typical mid-lifer's day:

1. Small breakfast (coffee + toast) → blood sugar spikes, then drops
2. Lunch with too many carbs → another spike, bigger drop
3. 3pm → blood sugar crashes → "I NEED SUGAR NOW"
4. Sugar fix → temporary relief → crash again at 5pm

You're not weak. You're riding a blood sugar roller coaster designed by your food choices.

---

## The 3-Day Sugar Reset

### What to Cut (for 3 days)
- Added sugar (soda, candy, cookies, pastries)
- Refined carbs (white bread, white rice, regular pasta)
- Sugary coffee drinks (lattes, frappes, sweetened creamers)

### What to Add (for 3 days)
- Protein at every meal (see Lesson 1)
- Whole fruit when you crave sweet (apple, berries, orange)
- Sparkling water with lemon for the "I want something" feeling

### The 10-Minute Rule
When the craving hits at 3pm, set a timer for 10 minutes. Do something else (walk, stretch, call someone). If you still want the sugar after 10 minutes, have it. Most of the time, the craving passes.

---

## The Truth

You don't have to quit sugar forever. That's not realistic. But you do need to be in control of it instead of the other way around. Three days of reduced sugar resets your taste buds and breaks the dopamine loop.
