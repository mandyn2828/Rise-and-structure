## The Four Pillars of Sleep Environment

### 1. Darkness
Melatonin (the sleep hormone) is produced in the dark and suppressed by light.

**Audit your room tonight:**
- Can you see your hand in front of your face? If yes, it's too bright.
- Cover or unplug LED lights (chargers, alarm clocks, smoke detectors)
- Use blackout curtains (or a $15 sleep mask)
- Close the door (hallway light leaks)

**Cost of fix:** $0-$30

### 2. Temperature
Your body needs to drop 1-2°F to fall asleep and stay asleep.

**Optimal range:** 65-68°F (18-20°C)

**If you can't control the thermostat:**
- Thinner blankets
- Sleep with feet out (they regulate temperature)
- A cooling pillow
- A fan (white noise + air circulation)

### 3. Noise
Sudden noises (car honk, phone ping, door slam) spike cortisol and pull you out of deep sleep.

**Solutions:**
- White noise machine or app ($0-30)
- Earplugs ($5)
- Close the window if it's noisy

### 4. Comfort
Your mattress, pillow, and sheets matter more than you think.

**Quick fixes:**
- A pillow that keeps your neck aligned (not too high, not too flat)
- 100% cotton or bamboo sheets (breathable)
- Replace your mattress if it's >8 years old

## The $100 Sleep Upgrade

If you can invest $100 in your sleep, buy these in order:

1. **Blackout curtains** ($25) — biggest impact for the least money
2. **Sleep mask** ($10-15) — great for travel and persistent light
3. **White noise machine** ($30) — blocks disturbances
4. **Cooling pillow** ($35) — if you sleep hot
