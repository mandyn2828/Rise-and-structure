## What You'll Learn
The difference between good stress (growth) and bad stress (damage) — and how to tell which one you're experiencing.

---

## Not All Stress Is Bad

Stress gets a bad reputation. But the right kind of stress makes you stronger.

| Good Stress (Eustress) | Bad Stress (Distress) |
|----------------------|----------------------|
| A challenging workout | Chronic work pressure |
| Learning a new skill | Financial uncertainty |
| Public speaking | Relationship conflict |
| A tight deadline | Constant overwhelm |
| You recover | You don't recover |

**The difference?** Recovery. Good stress comes in waves. Bad stress is a constant tide.

---

## The Stress Bucket

Imagine you have a bucket. Every day, stressors fill it:

- Traffic (adds)
- Work pressure (adds)
- Poor sleep (adds)
- Skiping meals (adds)
- Family obligations (adds)

Every day, recovery empties it:

- Good sleep (drains)
- Movement (drains)
- Connection (drains)
- Time alone (drains)
- Fun (drains)

**When the bucket overflows, you crash.**

---

## The Stress Audit

Answer these three questions:

1. **What's filling my bucket right now?** (List everything stressful)
2. **What's draining it?** (List everything that helps you recover)
3. **What's the one thing I can add or remove today to keep the bucket from overflowing?**

---

## Today's Action

Write down 5 things that are stressing you right now. Next to each, write whether it's eustress (challenging but manageable) or distress (overwhelming and constant).

For anything labeled distress, ask: *"Is there one small step I can take to reduce this?"*
