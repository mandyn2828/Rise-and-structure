## The Stat That Changed Everything

Most "mid-afternoon fatigue" is actually dehydration.

Your body is 60% water. Your brain is 73% water. Even a 2% drop in hydration levels causes:

- Reduced focus and concentration
- Increased perception of effort (everything feels harder)
- Headaches and irritability
- Slower metabolism

And here's the kicker: **by the time you feel thirsty, you're already dehydrated.**

---

## The 2-Liter System

You need roughly 2.5 liters of water per day (more if you exercise, less if you're smaller). Here's how to hit it without tracking:

### The 3-Bottle System

1. **Morning bottle (750ml)** — Fill it at night. Drink it within 1 hour of waking. Before coffee.

2. **Desk bottle (1L)** — Fill when you get to work. Finish by lunch. Refill. Finish by 4pm.

3. **Evening bottle (500ml)** — Sip between dinner and bedtime. Stop 1 hour before sleep to avoid nighttime bathroom trips.

**Total: 2.25L minimum. Easy.**

---

## The Coffee Rule

Coffee is not hydration — it's a diuretic. Every cup of coffee = you need an additional 250ml of water to offset it.

**Rule:** For every cup of coffee, drink one glass of water first.

---

## Warning Signs You're Dehydrated
- You feel hungry when you actually aren't (thirst mimics hunger)
- 3pm brain fog
- Dark yellow urine
- Dry lips or skin
- You can't focus
