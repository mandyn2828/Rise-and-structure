## What You'll Learn
Why intense exercise can actually make stress worse — and the type of movement that truly clears cortisol.

---

## The Mistake Most People Make

When they're stressed, they think: "I need to hit the gym hard. Sweat it out. Go all out."

Here's the problem: **intense exercise increases cortisol.** If you're already chronically stressed — already high cortisol — adding more intense stress (even "good" stress) pushes you further over the edge.

---

## Zone 2 Is the Answer

Zone 2 cardio is movement at a pace where you can still hold a conversation.

| Zone | Effort | Talking | Cortisol Effect |
|------|--------|---------|----------------|
| 1 | Very easy | Full conversation | Lowers cortisol |
| 2 | Easy | Can talk in sentences | Lowers cortisol |
| 3 | Moderate | Short phrases | Neutral |
| 4 | Hard | Single words | May increase |
| 5 | All-out | Can't talk | Increases |

**For stress relief:** Stay in Zone 1-2.

---

## The 20-Minute Reset

### What to Do
- Walking (outdoors is best)
- Easy cycling
- Gentle yoga or stretching
- Swimming at a relaxed pace
- Slow jogging

### The Protocol
- 20 minutes minimum (to clear cortisol from the bloodstream)
- No headphones for the first 10 minutes
- Let your mind wander — don't force positive thinking
- After 20 minutes, check in: how do I feel?

---

## Outdoor Bonus

Walking outside lowers cortisol 30% more than walking inside. Sunlight + nature + movement = the trifecta of stress relief.

**Today's mission:** Walk outside for 20 minutes. No phone. No podcasts. Just walk.
