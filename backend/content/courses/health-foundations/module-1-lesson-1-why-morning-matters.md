# The Morning Reset — Lesson 1: Why Morning Matters

**Module 1 of 4 | Day 1 | Time: 10 min**

---

## What You'll Learn
Why the first 10 minutes of your day determine the next 16 hours — and how to stop letting your phone set the tone.

---

## The Problem

Most people wake up and immediately:

1. Check their phone (other people's priorities)
2. Feel anxious about the day ahead
3. Rush into reactivity before they've even had water

Your morning is the only time of day when you control the agenda. By 9am, the world starts demanding things. By noon, you're reacting. The question isn't "will I be busy today?" — it's "whose agenda will I be serving?"

The morning reset takes back control.

---

## The Science

When you wake up, your brain is in a theta state — highly suggestible. This is why the first thing you consume (phone, news, email) sets your emotional tone for the day.

By delaying phone-checking by even 10 minutes, you:

- Reduce cortisol spikes by up to 30%
- Increase likelihood of making healthy choices later
- Build self-trust — you kept a promise to yourself

---

## Your Morning Reset Protocol

### 3 Rules Before You Touch Your Phone

1. **Hydrate** — Drink 500ml (2 glasses) of water. Your brain is dehydrated after 7-8 hours of sleep. Caffeine before water = starting the day in deficit.

2. **Move** — 2 minutes of gentle movement. Not a workout. Just waking up your body (shoulder rolls, neck stretches, a few deep squats).

3. **Set an intention** — One sentence: *"Today, I will __________."* Not a to-do. A feeling or focus.

That's it. Total time: 5 minutes. The phone can wait.
