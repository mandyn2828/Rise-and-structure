# Stress to Strength — Lesson 4: The Tension-Release Protocol

**Module 3 of 4 | Day 11 | Time: 10 min**

---

## What You'll Learn
A systematic way to release physical tension you didn't even know you were holding.

---

## The Problem

Stress doesn't just live in your head. It lives in your body:

- **Jaw:** Clenched (you might do this without noticing)
- **Shoulders:** Hiked up toward your ears
- **Hands:** Fists partially clenched
- **Stomach:** Tight (you're bracing)
- **Hips:** Tight from sitting
- **Feet:** Curled or tense

Most people walk around holding tension in 3-4 areas simultaneously, 24/7. No wonder you're exhausted.

---

## The Protocol: Progressive Relaxation

### Step 1: Body Scan (2 min)
Lie down or sit comfortably. Close your eyes. Starting at your toes, mentally scan upward to your scalp. Where do you feel tightness? Just notice. Don't try to change it yet.

### Step 2: Tense-Release Sequence (5 min)

For each area, tense HARD for 5 seconds, then release completely. Feel the difference between tense and relaxed.

| Area | How to Tense | Hold | Release |
|------|-------------|------|---------|
| Feet | Curl toes tightly | 5s | 10s |
| Legs | Squeeze quads and glutes | 5s | 10s |
| Stomach | Tighten abs | 5s | 10s |
| Hands | Make tight fists | 5s | 10s |
| Shoulders | Shrug to ears | 5s | 10s |
| Jaw | Clench teeth | 5s | 10s |
| Face | Squeeze whole face | 5s | 10s |

### Step 3: Deep Rest (3 min)
Lie still. Scan your body again. Notice the difference. Let yourself be heavy. Breathe.

---

## Why This Works

Tension is a habit your nervous system has learned. By consciously tensing and releasing, you're teaching it a new pattern: *I can let go.*

After 5-7 days of this, your baseline tension level drops permanently.

---

## When to Use

- End of workday (transition from work mode)
- Before bed (improves sleep onset)
- After a stressful conversation
- Any time you notice your shoulders are up
