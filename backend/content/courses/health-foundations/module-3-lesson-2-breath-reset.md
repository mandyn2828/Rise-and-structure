## What You'll Learn
A 60-second breathing technique to lower your heart rate and calm your nervous system — usable anywhere, anytime.

---

## The Technique: Box Breathing

Used by Navy SEALs, first responders, and anyone who needs to stay calm under pressure.

### The Pattern

```
Inhale (4 counts) → Hold (4 counts) → Exhale (4 counts) → Hold (4 counts)
```

Visualize a box:

```
    ┌─── 4 INHALE ───┐
    │                 │
   4 HOLD           4 HOLD
    │                 │
    └─── 4 EXHALE ───┘
```

---

## When to Use It

| Situation | Why It Works |
|-----------|-------------|
| Before a stressful meeting | Lowers anticipatory anxiety |
| When you're angry | Activates parasympathetic system |
| Can't sleep | Shifts brain from beta to alpha waves |
| Racing thoughts | Forces focus on one thing (breath) |
| After bad news | Stops the spiral before it starts |

---

## The Practice

### Step 1: Find Your Seat
Sit comfortably. Back straight but not rigid. Feet flat. Hands on thighs.

### Step 2: Empty Your Lungs
Exhale completely through your mouth.

### Step 3: The Box
- Inhale through your nose for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts (don't tighten — just pause)
- Exhale through your mouth for 4 counts
- Hold at empty for 4 counts

### Step 4: Repeat 5-10 Cycles

That's 60-90 seconds total.

---

## Why 4x4x4x4?

- **4-count inhale** fills your lungs completely
- **4-count hold** allows oxygen to fully saturate your blood
- **4-count exhale** activates the vagus nerve (your "calm down" nerve)
- **4-count empty hold** resets your CO2 tolerance

The longer exhale is the key. That's what tells your nervous system: "We're safe."
