**Module 2 of 4 | Day 5 | Time: 10 min**

## What You'll Learn
Why mid-lifers need more protein than you think — and how to hit your target without meal-prepping for hours.

---

## The Problem

Most mid-lifers are protein-deficient without realizing it. The signs:

- You're hungry an hour after eating
- You crave sugar in the afternoon
- Your energy crashes mid-day
- You're losing muscle even though you exercise
- Your nails are brittle, your hair is thinning

---

## The Number

**Target:** 0.7g of protein per pound of body weight

| Your Weight | Your Daily Target |
|-------------|------------------|
| 150 lbs | 105g |
| 175 lbs | 122g |
| 200 lbs | 140g |
| 225 lbs | 157g |
| 250 lbs | 175g |

**If you're not tracking:** This probably sounds like a lot. Most people eat 50-80g per day.

---

## How to Hit Your Number (Without Obsessing)

**Breakfast (30g):**
- 3 eggs (18g) + 1 cup Greek yogurt (15g) = 33g
- Or: Protein shake (25g) + 2 eggs (12g) = 37g

**Lunch (35g):**
- 6oz chicken breast (35g)
- Or: 1 can tuna (40g) mixed with avocado
- Or: 2 cups lentils (36g)

**Dinner (40g):**
- 8oz salmon (45g)
- Or: 8oz lean beef (46g)
- Or: 1.5 cups cottage cheese (40g) + veggies

**Total with minimal effort: ~100-120g**

If you're short, add a protein shake as a between-meal snack (25g, ~100 calories).

---

## The Simple Rule

**Every meal must have a protein source.** If a meal doesn't have obvious protein (chicken, fish, eggs, meat, tofu, legumes, yogurt), it's a snack, not a meal.
