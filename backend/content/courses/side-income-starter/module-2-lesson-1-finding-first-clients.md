# The Freelancer Path — Lesson 1: Finding Your First 3 Clients

**Module 2 of 6 | Day 4 | Time: 15 min**

---

## What You'll Learn
Where to find your first 3 clients — and how to approach them without feeling salesy.

---

## Where Clients Come From

| Source | Speed | Ease | Quality |
|--------|-------|------|---------|
| Your existing network | Fast | Easy | High |
| Freelance platforms (Upwork, Contra) | Medium | Medium | Medium |
| Cold outreach | Slow | Hard | Variable |
| Referrals | Fast | Easy | Highest |

**Start with your network.** Your first clients are people who already know, like, and trust you.

---

## The Network Script

### Step 1: Make a List
Write down 20 people you know. Don't judge who's "useful" — just list:

- Former coworkers
- Industry contacts (LinkedIn connections)
- Friends (especially ones who own businesses or know people who do)
- Family members who work
- Neighbors
- Anyone who's ever said "you're good at X"

### Step 2: The Message

Here's a template. Use your own words, but keep the structure:

> *"Hey [Name], hope you're doing well!*
>
> *I'm starting to take on [service] clients on the side. Since you know me and my work, I wanted to ask: do you know anyone who might need help with [service]? I'm offering a discounted rate for my first few clients to build my portfolio.*
>
> *No pressure if not, but I'd appreciate you keeping me in mind!"*

### Step 3: Send 5 Messages Today

Not 20. Not 3. Five. Today. Right after this lesson.

---

## Platform Approach (Upwork/Contra)

If your network doesn't work:

1. Create a profile that mentions a **specific problem** you solve
2. Search for jobs that mention that problem
3. Submit proposals that show you understand their EXACT situation
4. Apply to 3 jobs/day until you land one

**Tip:** Keep your first rates low. You're building a portfolio, not a retirement fund.
