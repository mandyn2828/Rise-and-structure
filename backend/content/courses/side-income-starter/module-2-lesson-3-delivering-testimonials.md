## What You'll Learn
How to over-deliver without over-working — and how to turn every project into a referral machine.

---

## The Over-Deliver Secret

Over-delivering doesn't mean working more hours. It means:

- **Under-promise** — Give a deadline that's 2 days later than you need. Deliver early.
- **Add one small surprise** — A bonus tip, a quick follow-up, a resource they didn't expect
- **Be easy to work with** — Fast communication, clear expectations, no drama

---

## The Testimonial Request

After every project, send this:

> *"Hi [Client], I really enjoyed working on [project]. Would you be open to sharing a quick testimonial? Just 2-3 sentences about your experience working with me. It helps other clients know what to expect."*

**Make it easy:** Give them a template:

> *"Working with [Name] was great. They [did specific thing] and the result was [specific result]. I'd recommend them to anyone who needs [service]."*

---

## The Referral Ask

After the testimonial:

> *"Thanks so much — I really appreciate it. One more favor: if you know anyone else who might need help with [service], I'd love an introduction. No pressure at all."*

**Pro tip:** Offer a referral bonus (one free hour, 10% off their next project) for every referral that turns into a client.

---

## The Post-Project Checklist

- ✅ Deliver on time (or early)
- ✅ Add one small surprise
- ✅ Send invoice
- ✅ Request testimonial
- ✅ Ask for referral
- ✅ Send thank-you note
- ✅ Add to portfolio
