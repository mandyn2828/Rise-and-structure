## What You'll Learn
How to set your rates — and why you should raise them sooner than you think.

---

## The Three Pricing Models

### 1. Hourly
- **Best for:** New freelancers, service work
- **Risk:** You cap your income by time
- **Starting point:** Your desired hourly * 2 (if you're working a job at $30/hr, charge $60/hr freelance)

### 2. Per Project
- **Best for:** Defined deliverables (logo, website, report)
- **Risk:** Scope creep (client adds more work)
- **Solution:** Define scope clearly in writing before starting

### 3. Value-Based
- **Best for:** Experienced freelancers
- **How it works:** Price based on the VALUE to the client, not your time
- **Example:** A $500 email sequence that generates $5,000 in sales is a bargain at $500

---

## The Rate Ramp

| Phase | Rate | Why |
|-------|------|-----|
| First 3 clients | Low (50-70% of target) | Build portfolio, get testimonials |
| 3-10 clients | Target rate | You have proof of quality |
| 10+ clients | Premium (1.5-2x target) | Demand > supply |

---

## The "Raise Your Rates" Trigger

Raise your rates when:

- You're turning down work
- You have more clients than you can handle
- You've been at the same rate for 6+ months
- A client says "you're worth more than you charge"
- You feel resentful doing the work for current pay

---

## The Price Objection Script

When a client says "that's more than I expected":

> *"I understand. My rates reflect the quality and reliability I bring. If it's outside your budget right now, I'd be happy to recommend a simpler solution or point you to someone who might be a better fit."*

Don't negotiate down. Offer less scope instead.
