## What You'll Learn
Systems for onboarding, delivering, and invoicing — so nothing falls through the cracks.

---

## The Client Lifecycle

```
Lead → Discovery Call → Onboarding → Delivery → Invoice → Follow-up → Repeat
```

At every stage, have a system.

---

## Onboarding System

When a new client says yes, send this within 1 hour:

1. **Welcome email** — "Thanks for trusting me. Here's what happens next."
2. **Brief / scope document** — Exactly what you'll deliver, by when, for how much
3. **Invoice** — Payment terms + link to pay
4. **Calendar link** — Book first session or kickoff call
5. **Prep form** — "To get the most from our work together, please [do X] before we start"

**Template this.** Never rewrite from scratch.

---

## Delivery System

For every project:

- [ ] Create project folder (Google Drive)
- [ ] Set deadlines in calendar (your internal deadlines, not client-facing)
- [ ] Complete work
- [ ] Review before sending (always wait 1 hour before hitting send)
- [ ] Send deliverable + brief summary
- [ ] Mark project as "Delivered" in your tracker

---

## Invoicing System

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| Invoice immediately after delivery | Time = momentum |
| Net 15 payment terms | 30 days is too long |
| Late fee after 7 days | Encourages prompt payment |
| Auto-reminders | Don't chase manually |

**Invoice template structure:**
- Your name/business
- Client name
- Date issued
- Description of work
- Amount due
- Payment link
- Due date
