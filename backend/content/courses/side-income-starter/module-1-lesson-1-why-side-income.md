## The Single-Income Trap

Most people operate on a single-income model:

```
Job → Salary → Expenses → (maybe) Savings → Repeat
```

The problem: If the job goes away, everything stops. One layoff, one injury, one economic downturn — and you're exposed.

A side income changes the equation:

```
Job → Salary → Expenses → Savings
Side Income → Extra → Investments → Freedom
```

**$500/month from a side income could mean:**
- Paying off debt 2x faster
- Actually saving for retirement
- Taking that trip you've postponed
- Quitting a job you hate (because you have a buffer)

## The Time Audit

Everyone says "I don't have time." But you have the same 168 hours per week as everyone else.

**Let's see where yours go:**

| Activity | Hours/Week |
|----------|-----------|
| Sleep (8h x 7) | 56 |
| Work (40h x 1) | 40 |
| Commute (1h x 5) | 5 |
| Meals (2h x 7) | 14 |
| Personal care (1.5h x 7) | 10 |
| Family responsibilities | varies |
| TV/social media/scrolling | ? |
| **Side income potential** | **???** |

Most people find 5-10 hours of "wasted time" per week (scrolling, TV, aimless browsing). That's enough to build a side income.

**Where will your 5 hours come from?**

## The $100 First Milestone

The first $100 you earn from side income is more important than the first $10,000. Why?

Because $100 proves it's possible. $100 turns a fantasy into a real possibility. $100 gives you the confidence to keep going.

**Your goal:** Make $100 in the next 30 days from something that's not your day job.
