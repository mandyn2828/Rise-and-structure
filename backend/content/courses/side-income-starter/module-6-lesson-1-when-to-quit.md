## What You'll Learn
How to know when it's time to go all-in — and how to do it without risking everything.

## The "Should I Quit?" Calculator

Don't rely on feelings. Use math.

**Your Side Income Stability Score:**

| Factor | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Side income covers 50%+ of expenses | +3 |
| 3+ months of consistent income | +2 |
| 3+ repeat clients | +2 |
| You have 6 months of savings | +2 |
| You have a waiting list | +2 |
| You enjoy it more than your job | +1 |
| Your day job is affecting your health | +1 |

**Score 10+?** Start planning your exit.
**Score 6-9?** Keep building — you're close.
**Score <6?** Stay the course. Keep building.

---

## The Transition Plan

Don't quit on a Friday and start on Monday. Plan a transition:

| Phase | Timeline | What Happens |
|-------|----------|-------------|
| Build | Months 1-6 | Side income alongside job |
| Bridge | Months 6-9 | Reduce day job hours (if possible) or take sabbatical |
| Leap | Month 9+ | Go full-time on your own |

---

## The Financial Safety Threshold

**Before you quit, you need:**
- 6 months of living expenses in savings
- Side income at 50% of your current salary
- A plan to fill the gap in 3-6 months

---

## The Emotional Readiness Check

Answer honestly:
1. "If my side income fails, can I get another job?" (Probably yes — you have skills)
2. "What's the worst that could happen?" (You go back to a job)
3. "What's the best that could happen?" (Freedom, fulfillment, control)
4. "What will I regret more — trying and failing, or never trying?"
