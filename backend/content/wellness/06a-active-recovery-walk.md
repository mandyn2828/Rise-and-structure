# Day 6A — Active Recovery Walk

**Tier: Starter | Time: 20-30 min | Week: Foundation**

## The Why
Recovery isn't laziness — it's when your body rebuilds. An active recovery walk keeps blood flowing, clears metabolic waste from muscles, and gives your brain a reset. This is the single highest-ROI activity for mid-life health.

## The Walk

### Before You Go
- Shoes you can walk comfortably in for 20+ min
- No headphones (or only one earbud) — let your brain process
- Leave your phone in your pocket unless you're tracking steps

### The Structure (20 min)

| Segment | Duration | Intention |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| Start slow | 2 min | Let body warm up, arms swing naturally |
| Brisk pace | 10 min | Breath quickens but you can still talk |
| Scenic pause | 3 min | Stop at a tree, view, or interesting spot. Breathe. Look. |
| Return pace | 3 min | Easy pace back |
| Cool down | 2 min | Walk slowly, shake out arms and legs |

### Walking Prompts (pick one for today)
- "What's one thing I'm carrying that I don't need to?"
- "Where did I feel joy this week?"
- "What's one problem I'm trying to solve — and what would I tell a friend in my situation?"

## Builder Variation (30-40 min)
- Walk at a pace that makes conversation slightly difficult
- Add 3 x 30-second "speed bursts" (fast as you can without running)
- End with 5 min of gentle stretching (calves, quads, hamstrings)

## Thriver Variation (45-60 min)
- Add hills or stairs
- Carry light hand weights or a weighted vest
- Practice "walking meditation" — synchronize breath with steps (4 steps in, 4 steps out)

## Tracking Prompt
> "How did I feel before the walk? During? After? What changed?"

## Habit Anchor
Keep walking shoes by the door. If it's convenient enough, you'll do it.
