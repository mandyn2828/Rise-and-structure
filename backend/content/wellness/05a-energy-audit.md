# Day 5A — Energy Audit

**Tier: Builder | Time: 15 min | Week: Foundation**

## The Why
You can't fix what you don't measure. Most mid-lifers run on autopilot, wondering why they're exhausted by 3pm. This audit identifies your energy leaks so you can plug them.

## The Exercise

### Part 1: Yesterday's Timeline (5 min)
Write down yesterday hour by hour (approximate is fine). For each block, note your energy level (1-10) and what you were doing.

| Time | Activity | Energy (1-10) |
|------|----------|---------------|
| 6-7am | | |
| 7-8am | | |
| 8-12pm | | |
| 12-1pm | | |
| 1-3pm | | |
| 3-5pm | | |
| 5-7pm | | |
| 7-10pm | | |
| 10pm+ | | |

### Part 2: Identify Patterns (5 min)
Look at your chart. Answer:
1. When did energy peak? What were you doing?
2. When did energy crash? What preceded it?
3. What patterns emerge? (Post-meal slump? Mid-afternoon crash? Late-night second wind?)

### Part 3: One Energy Fix (5 min)
Pick ONE change for today:
- **If post-meal slump:** reduce carb load at lunch, add protein
- **If mid-afternoon crash:** schedule a 10-min walk at 2pm
- **If chronic low energy:** check: did you eat breakfast? Did you drink enough water?
- **If brain fog:** reduce multitasking, do deep work in 45-min blocks

## Starter Variation
- Skip the full timeline — just note 3 energy peaks and 3 energy valleys from yesterday
- Pick one fix from the list above

## Thriver Add-on
- Track TODAY in real time (set a phone alarm every 2 hours)
- Eliminate one known energy drain (the meeting that could be an email, the news habit, the 3pm candy bar)
- Add a strategic 20-min nap (before 3pm, 20 min max)

## Tracking Prompt
> "What's the #1 thing that drained my energy today? What's the #1 thing that fueled me?"

## Habit Anchor
Keep a sticky note on your monitor: "How is my energy right now?" — it rewires awareness.
