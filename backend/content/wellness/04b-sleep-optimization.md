# Day 4B — Sleep Optimization

**Tier: Builder | Time: 15 min | Week: Build**

## The Why
You've cleaned up the basics (wind-down, screen reduction). Now let's optimize. Sleep quality matters more than sleep quantity. Six hours of deep sleep beats eight hours of restless sleep.

## The Optimization Stack

### 1. Light Management
- **Morning:** Get 10 min of natural sunlight within 30 min of waking. This sets your circadian clock.
- **Afternoon:** Blue light is fine (it helps alertness)
- **Evening:** Dim lights after sunset. Use warm/red lights in the last 2 hours.
- **Night:** Blackout room or sleep mask. Cover LED lights on electronics.

**Today's Action:** Stand outside (no sunglasses) for 10 min this morning.

### 2. Temperature Optimization
- Room temp: 65-68°F (18-20°C) — cooler = deeper sleep
- Hot shower 60-90 min before bed (the temperature drop after triggers sleep onset)
- If you wake at 3am feeling overheated: lighter blanket, thinner pajamas

### 3. The Caffeine Curfew
Caffeine has a 5-6 hour half-life. Coffee at 4pm means half of it is still in your system at 10pm.

**Today's Action:** Move your last caffeine to 12pm (or earlier). Replace afternoon coffee with decaf, herbal tea, or sparkling water.

### 4. Alcohol Awareness
Alcohol fragments sleep. One drink reduces REM sleep by ~20-30%. A few drinks can cut restorative deep sleep in half.

**Today's Action:** If you drink alcohol tonight, note: how many drinks? How was sleep quality? Experiment: see what happens with zero alcohol.

## Starter Variation
- Pick just ONE optimization: sunlight in the morning
- Move caffeine cutoff to 2pm instead of 12pm

## Thriver Variation
- Wear blue-light blocking glasses for 2 hours before bed
- Tape your mouth at night (mouth taping promotes nasal breathing — higher nitric oxide, deeper sleep)
- Use a weighted blanket (10-15% of body weight)

## Tracking Prompt
> "What's the single biggest factor hurting my sleep right now? (Late caffeine? Alcohol? Room temp? Light? Mind racing?)"

## Habit Anchor
Coffee goes with a full glass of water. Every time. Caffeine + dehydration = worse sleep.
