# Day 7B — Intentional Rest

**Tier: All levels | Time: Variable | Week: Build**

## The Why
Rest is not the absence of work — it's an active practice. In a culture that worships busyness, intentional rest is a radical act. It's also essential: without true rest, your body accumulates a cortisol debt that destroys sleep, mood, and health.

## The Practice

### The Rest Spectrum

| Type | Example | Time |
|------|---------|------|
| Physical Rest | Nap, lie in hammock, stretching | 20-60 min |
| Mental Rest | No decisions, no screens, no inputs | 30-60 min |
| Social Rest | Quality time with people who energize you | 1-2 hrs |
| Spiritual Rest | Time in nature, meditation, prayer, purpose reflection | 20-30 min |
| Sensory Rest | Dim lights, no noise, no podcasts, no music | 15-30 min |
| Emotional Rest | Permission to feel, journal, talk, cry, laugh | 30-60 min |

### Today's Prescription

Pick 3 types of rest from the spectrum above. Schedule them into your day.

**Example:**
- Morning: 10 min sensory rest (sit in dark room, no phone, no sound)
- Afternoon: 30 min physical rest (lie on couch, no scrolling — just resting)
- Evening: 1 hour social rest (dinner with family, no phones, no work talk)

### What NOT to do
- Do NOT call "scrolling on the couch" rest. It's not. Your brain is still processing.
- Do NOT call "watching a show" rest unless you are FULLY present and not multitasking.
- Do NOT check email or Slack at all. The world survives without you for one day.

## Starter Variation
- Schedule a 20-min tech-free break
- Lie down somewhere comfortable and do a body scan (Day 3A)

## Thriver Variation
- Practice a full "media blackout" — no news, social media, or email for the entire day
- Take an afternoon nap (20 min, before 3pm)
- Have a conversation with someone you love where you ONLY listen (don't plan your response)

## Weekly Closeout

Take 5 min now. Answer:

1. What was the best part of this week?
2. What was the hardest part?
3. What do I want NEXT week to feel like?
4. What's ONE thing I can do Sunday evening to set next week up for success?

## Tracking Prompt
> "Did I truly rest today? Or just not work? What's the difference?"

## Habit Anchor
Sunday = Rest Day. Block it on your calendar. When people ask for your time, say "I'm unavailable Sundays." Protect it like a CEO protects their calendar.
