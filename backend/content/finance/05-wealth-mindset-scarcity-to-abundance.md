# Day 5 — Wealth Mindset: The Scarcity-to-Abundance Shift

## The Principle
Money is emotional. Your childhood money story, your parents' relationship with money, and your past financial mistakes all shape how you earn, spend, and save today. Until you update the story, no budgeting app will save you.

## The Action: The Money Story Rewrite (15 min)

### Step 1: Trace Your Money Script (5 min)
Complete these sentences quickly (first answer that comes to mind):
1. "Money is ______."
2. "Rich people are ______."
3. "I deserve [much/more/some/less] money because ______."
4. "My parents taught me that money ______."
5. "The most painful financial moment in my life was ______."

### Step 2: Identify the Story (5 min)
Read your answers. What's the narrative?
- "Money is scarce. You have to work hard for it and hold on tight."
- "Money is dirty/complicated/not for people like me."
- "I'm bad with money. It's just who I am."
- "I don't deserve to earn more."

These are not facts. They are stories you learned. They can be rewritten.

### Step 3: Write a New Story (5 min)
Write one sentence that counters your old story:
- Old: "Money is scarce."
- New: "Money flows. I can earn it, enjoy it, and share it."

- Old: "I'm bad with money."
- New: "I'm learning to manage money well, one step at a time."

## The Wealth-Building Habit: Pay Yourself First (Forever)

**The Rule:** Every time you get paid, 10% goes to you before anyone else.
- Put it in a savings account, an investment account, or your emergency fund
- Never touch it for anything except: emergencies, retirement, or a wealth-building investment

This is non-negotiable. $10/week at 7% return = $24,000 in 20 years. $100/week = $240,000.

## Starter Goal
Write down one money belief you're ready to let go of. Say it out loud: "I release the belief that [old belief]."

## Builder Goal
Set up automated "pay yourself first" transfer. Even $10 per paycheck.

## Thriver Goal
Calculate your "financial freedom number": how much monthly passive income would free you from worrying about money? Then calculate how much capital you'd need to generate that (multiply by 300 for a 4% withdrawal rate).

## Resource
**Book:** *The Psychology of Money* by Morgan Housel (shortest, most impactful finance book you'll read).

## Tracking Prompt
> "What's one money belief that's been holding me back? What would I do differently if I truly believed I deserved more?"

## Coach's Note
Your income is capped by your self-worth, not your skills. Do the inner work. The outer results will follow.
