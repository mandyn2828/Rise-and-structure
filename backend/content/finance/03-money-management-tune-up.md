# Day 3 — Money Management: The Mid-Life Money Tune-Up

## The Principle
You don't need a complex budget. You need **visibility** — knowing where your money actually goes — and **one automated system** so you never have to think about it again.

## The Action: The 30-Minute Money Tune-Up (20 min)

### Step 1: Calculate Your "Burn Rate" (5 min)
Look at your bank statement from last month. Add up:
- Fixed costs (rent/mortgage, utilities, insurance, subscriptions, debt min payments)
- Variable costs (food, gas, entertainment, shopping)
- ONE-TIME expenses

Write down: **$______ total spent last month**

### Step 2: The 50/30/20 Check (5 min)
Compare your numbers:
- **50% Needs** — housing, utilities, groceries, insurance, minimum debt payments
- **30% Wants** — dining out, travel, subscriptions, hobbies, shopping
- **20% Savings/Debt** — extra debt payments, emergency fund, investments

Your actual split: ______ / ______ / ______

**If wants > 30%:** Pick one category to cut by 20% next month.
**If savings < 20%:** Automate $X/month to a separate account. Start with whatever you can manage.

### Step 3: Find One "Money Leak" (5 min)
Look at your subscriptions. Most people have $50-100/month in forgotten subscriptions.

| Subscription | Cost | Do I use it? | Keep/Cancel |
|-------------|------|-------------|-------------|
| | | | |
| | | | |

**Cancel one today.**

### Step 4: One Automation (5 min)
Pick ONE:
- Set up automatic transfer of $X to savings on payday
- Set up autopay for all fixed bills (no late fees)
- Set up a credit card alert for any charge over $100

## Starter Goal
Just calculate your burn rate. That's it. Awareness is the first step.

## Builder Goal
Complete all 4 steps. Cancel one subscription.

## Thriver Goal
Review your investment account fees. Are you paying more than 0.5% in fees? If so, research low-fee index funds.

## Resource
**Tool:** YNAB (You Need A Budget) — free 34-day trial. Or use a simple Google Sheet with 3 columns: Income, Bills, Everything Else.

## Tracking Prompt
> "What's one financial number I've been avoiding? What's different now that I know it?"

## Coach's Note
You don't need to be a financial genius. You need three things: visibility, automation, and one month of expenses in the bank. Start there.
