# Day 3 (Week B) — Money Management: The Emergency Fund Challenge

## The Principle
An emergency fund is not boring — it's freedom. With $3,000 in the bank, you can survive a car breakdown, a medical bill, or a layoff without going into debt. With nothing, every surprise is a crisis.

## The Action: Build Your Safety Net (15 min)

### Step 1: Know Your Number
- **Minimum viable fund:** 1 month of essential expenses
- **Comfortable fund:** 3 months of expenses
- **Sleep-well fund:** 6 months of expenses

Your essential monthly expenses: $______
Your target number: $______

### Step 2: Find the Money
Where can you find $X to start building?

| Source | Amount |

| Skip 1 coffee/drink/week | $______/month |
| Side gig (see Day 1 lesson) | $______/month |
| Reduce one bill (call to negotiate) | $______/month |

**Today's Immediate Action:** Sell ONE item you haven't used in 6 months. Put that money into your emergency fund. (Facebook Marketplace, eBay, or Craigslist. Price it to sell.)

### Step 3: The Automation
Set up one automatic transfer:
- $______ from each paycheck → separate savings account (don't touch it)
- Name the account "Freedom Fund" or "Sleep Well Account"

## Starter Goal
Open a separate high-yield savings account for your emergency fund. (Ally, Marcus, or Discover — all free.)

## Builder Goal
Sell one item. Put it in the fund. Automate $20/week.

## Thriver Goal
Automate 10% of income to the fund until you hit 3 months of expenses. Then redirect to investments.

## Tracking Prompt
> "If I lost my income today, how many days could I survive before financial panic? What's the ONE thing I can do this week to extend that by 30 days?"

## Coach's Note
Emergency fund isn't exciting. Neither is a seatbelt. But when you need it, nothing else matters. Build it before you invest. Build it before you splurge. Build it so you can sleep.
