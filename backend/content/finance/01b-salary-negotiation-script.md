# Day 1 (Week B) — Income Building: The Salary Negotiation Script

## The Principle
"Not asking" is the most expensive mistake you'll make this decade. One negotiation conversation can earn you more than a decade of coupons. 70% of people who negotiate get something. Most people never ask.

## The Action: Prepare Your Negotiation (20 min)

### Step 1: Know Your Market Value (10 min)
Research what someone with your role, experience, and location earns:
- Check Glassdoor, LinkedIn Salary, and Levels.fyi
- Find 3-5 data points
- Write down: **Your target number** (the number you'd be thrilled with)

### Step 2: Build Your Case (5 min)
List 3 specific accomplishments from the last 6-12 months:
1. What you did
2. The measurable impact (money saved, revenue generated, time saved, problems solved)
3. Why this proves you're worth more

### Step 3: The Script (5 min)
Practice saying this OUT LOUD:
> "I've really enjoyed working here and I'm excited about [specific project/company initiative]. Based on my contributions — [mention 1 key accomplishment] — and my market research showing the range for this role is [$X-$Y], I'm asking for a base salary of [$Z]. Does that seem fair to you?"

**Key rules:**
- Give a range where YOUR number is the bottom (ask for $85-95k if you want $85k)
- Let them speak first after you ask
- If they say no: "I understand budget constraints. What would it take to revisit this in 6 months?"
- Always get the offer in writing

## Starter Goal
Research your market value. Just know the number.

## Builder Goal
Write your case. Practice the script 3x.

## Thriver Goal
Schedule the meeting. Send the calendar invite this week.

## Resource
**Tool:** [Know Your Worth](https://www.glassdoor.com/know-your-worth) calculator on Glassdoor.

## Tracking Prompt
> "What's the number I'm worth? What's stopping me from asking for it?"

## Coach's Note
You are not being greedy. You are being fair. The company has a budget for your role. If you don't ask, that money goes to someone else — or stays in their pocket. Ask.
