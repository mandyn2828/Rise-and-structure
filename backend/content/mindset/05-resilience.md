# Day 5 — Resilience

## Morning Prompt
> "You have survived 100% of your worst days so far. You are more resilient than you give yourself credit for."

## Reflection (Write for 5 min)

1. What's the hardest thing I've overcome in my life? How did I get through it?
2. What's one current struggle that feels impossible? What would I tell a friend in the same situation?
3. What's one way I've already changed or grown from this difficult season?
4. Who or what has been my anchor during tough times?
5. What's one strength I have that I rarely acknowledge?

## Practice: The Resilience Timeline

Draw a simple timeline of your life. Mark 3-5 significant challenges you've faced. Next to each, write:
1. What happened
2. How I responded
3. What I learned
4. How I'm different because of it

**Then write this at the bottom:**
> "I am the person who has survived [challenge 1], navigated [challenge 2], and grown through [challenge 3]. The current difficulty is one more chapter I will survive and learn from."

## The "Reframing" Technique

When you catch yourself in a negative thought today, apply this reframe:

| Automatic Thought | Reframe |
|------------------|---------|
| "I can't do this." | "I haven't learned how yet. What's the smallest first step?" |
| "This always happens to me." | "This has happened before and I got through it. What worked then?" |
| "I'm not good enough." | "I'm comparing my behind-the-scenes to someone else's highlight reel." |
| "It's too late for me." | "The best time to plant a tree was 20 years ago. The second best time is now." |

## Affirmation (Repeat 3x today)
> "I am not defined by what happens to me. I am defined by how I respond. And I can choose my response."

## Evening Check-in (2 min)
> "What challenged me today? How did I respond? What would I do differently if I could redo it? What did I learn?"

## Coach's Note
Resilience isn't about being unbreakable — it's about knowing you can be broken and still rebuild. You've done it before. You'll do it again. The strength you developed through past struggles is still with you. Use it.
