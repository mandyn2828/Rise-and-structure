# Day 2 — Self-Compassion

## Morning Prompt
> "You would never talk to a friend the way you talk to yourself. Try talking to yourself like someone you love."

## Reflection (Write for 5 min)

1. What's the harshest thing I've said to myself recently?
2. Where did I learn that voice? (Parent? Boss? Partner? Culture?)
3. If a close friend told me they felt the same way, what would I say to them?
4. What would be different if I treated myself with the same compassion I show others?
5. What's one mistake I'm still punishing myself for? Is it time to forgive?

## Practice: The Compassion Letter

Write a brief letter to yourself (3-5 sentences) about ONE area where you've been hard on yourself. Write it with the same kindness you'd offer a close friend.

**Template:**
> "Dear [Your Name],
> I know you've been struggling with [issue]. I want you to know that [validation — it makes sense you feel this way, you're doing your best, etc.]. You deserve [kindness/patience/a break]. I'm proud of you for [one genuine thing].
> With love, [Your Name]"

Read it aloud. Yes, out loud. It will feel awkward. Do it anyway.

## Affirmation (Repeat 3x today)
> "I am allowed to be a work in progress. I am doing the best I can with what I have."

## Evening Check-in (2 min)
> "When was I kindest to myself today? When was I harshest? What would I tell a friend if they were in my shoes?"

## Coach's Note
The voice in your head is not the truth — it's a habit. You can change the habit. Start by noticing when it's cruel, and asking: "Would I say this to someone I love?" If not, don't say it to yourself.
