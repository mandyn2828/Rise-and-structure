# Day 4 — Emotional Awareness

## Morning Prompt
> "You can't change what you don't acknowledge. Today, name what you're feeling without judging it."

## Reflection (Write for 5 min)

1. What's the dominant emotion I'm feeling right now? (Just one word.)
2. Where do I feel that emotion in my body? (Chest tightness? Knot in stomach? Heavy shoulders?)
3. What triggered this feeling? (Be specific: a thought, a conversation, something you saw.)
4. What does this emotion want me to know? (Emotions are messengers. What's the message?)
5. What's one emotion I've been avoiding recently? What would happen if I just let myself feel it for 5 minutes?

## Practice: The Emotional Labeling Exercise

When you feel a strong emotion today, pause and label it.

**The Labeling Formula:**
> "I feel [emotion]. I feel it in my [body part]. It is [intensity 1-10]. I don't need to fix it. I just need to notice it."

**Examples:**
- "I feel anxiety. I feel it in my chest. It's a 6. I don't need to fix it. I just need to notice it."
- "I feel anger. I feel it in my jaw. It's a 7. I don't need to fix it. I just need to notice it."

**Why this works:** Labeling emotions activates the prefrontal cortex (thinking brain) and calms the amygdala (reaction brain). It literally rewires your response to stress.

## Today's Challenge
Set 3 phone alarms with the label "Pause." When it rings, take 2 deep breaths and ask: "What am I feeling right now?"

## Affirmation (Repeat 3x today)
> "My emotions are not my enemy. They are signals. I can feel them without being controlled by them."

## Evening Check-in (2 min)
> "What emotions did I experience today? Which one was strongest? What did I learn by noticing it instead of suppressing it?"

## Coach's Note
Most mid-lifers were taught to suppress emotions ("suck it up," "calm down," "stop crying"). Suppressed emotions don't disappear — they appear as tension, insomnia, irritability, or numbing behaviors (drinking, scrolling, overeating). Name them. Feel them. They pass.
